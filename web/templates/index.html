    Hora of the hour

    Ch 1.3.11 · The day from sunrise to next sunrise is split into 24 horas (~1 h each). Hora 1 is ruled by the weekday lord; subsequent horas follow decreasing speed: Saturn → Jupiter → Mars → Sun → Venus → Mercury → Moon (repeating).

    Lunar month (maasa)

    Ch 1.3.9 · A lunar month runs new-moon to new-moon. Its name comes from the rasi in which Sun and Moon conjoin at the start, and reflects the constellation where Moon is likely on full-moon day.

    Tithi & Paksha

    Ch 1.3.8 · A tithi is a 12° slice of (Moon − Sun). 30 tithis make a lunar month: tithis 1–15 form Sukla Paksha (waxing), 16–30 form Krishna Paksha (waning).

    Yoga & Karana

    Ch 1.3.9–10 · Yoga = (Sun°+Moon°) / 800′ (27 total). Karana = half a tithi (11 canonical, with 7 moving types that repeat through the month and 4 fixed at the boundaries).

    Panchaanga Muhurta

    Ch 1.3.12 · The five limbs (Tithi · Vaara · Nakshatra · Yoga · Karana) are judged together to estimate the overall tone of a moment. Heuristic only — specific activities (marriage, travel, surgery, …) layer additional rules on top.

    Solar Calendar

    Ch 1.3.7 · In the solar calendar, one day equals 1° of Sun's (sidereal) motion and one month equals 30° — so the year is 360 solar days long, starting at Mesha Sankranti (Sun enters Aries, ~14 April).
